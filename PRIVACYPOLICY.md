# QRCanvas Privacy Policy

Developer: Old Alex Hub

Effective date: May 16, 2026

Contact: info@oldalexhub.com

## Data collection

QRCanvas does not collect personal data. The app does not use accounts, subscriptions, tracking, cloud sync, or backend services.

## Advertising

QRCanvas displays ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may use device identifiers (such as the Android Advertising ID) to serve contextual ads. QRCanvas requests non-personalized ads only and does not pass personal data to AdMob. For more information on how Google uses data in ads, visit https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Users can opt out of interest-based ads through their device settings under Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization.

## Local storage

QR content, saved QR projects, templates, and app settings are stored locally on the user's device. This information remains on the device unless the user chooses to export or share a PNG image or JSON backup.

## Internet usage

QRCanvas works offline for all QR code features (create, save, edit, export, import, delete). An internet connection is used only to load ads via Google AdMob.

## Permissions

QRCanvas does not require camera, contacts, or location permission. The app does not request external storage permission for normal export because PNG and JSON exports use the local Android share flow/cache.

For location QR codes, users manually enter coordinates or paste a map URL. QRCanvas does not access the device location.

## Wi-Fi QR note

Wi-Fi QR codes may contain network names and passwords entered by the user. That content remains local unless the user exports or shares the QR image or JSON backup.

## Children's privacy

QRCanvas is a general utility app and does not knowingly collect information from children.

## Data deletion

Users can delete individual QR projects from the saved library. Users can also reset all QRCanvas local data from Settings. Resetting app data removes QRCanvas projects and preferences stored by this app on the device.

## Disclaimer

The app creates QR codes from user-entered information but cannot guarantee that every scanner or device will read every customized design. Users should test important QR designs before printing or distributing them.
